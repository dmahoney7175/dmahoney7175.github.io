Were the Phoenix Suns Playoff-Worthy in 2014?

Author

Published

The 2013-2014 NBA season was filled with unforgettable storylines: LeBron James played his final season with the Miami Heat, and the San Antonio Spurs beat the Heat to win their fifth NBA Championship in a Finals rematch.

However, people need to remember how competitive the Western Conference was during this dynamic NBA season. Eight teams won 49 games or more, and the 9th seed won 48 games but missed the playoffs.

That team was the Phoenix Suns. In a shocking turnaround, the Suns went from a sad 25 wins in 2013 to an impressive 48 wins in 2014, surpassing expectations.

This team should have made the 2014 playoffs.

The numbers tell the story!

Let’s start with net rating, a stat in the NBA that calculates point differential across 100 possessions. Good teams have a higher net rating, while bad teams have a lower net rating.

Here is the net rating of every Western Conference team during the 2013-2014 NBA season.

Phoenix finished with the seventh-best net rating in the Western Conference in 2014, posting a rating of 2.6. This was higher than the Memphis Grizzlies and the Dallas Mavericks, two teams that ultimately made the playoffs.
The Suns’ success was driven by their elite offense, ranking in the Top 10 in several offensive categories.

They averaged 105.2 points per game, placing 7th in the NBA, and recorded an offensive rating of 108.2, which ranked 8th in the league. Offensive rating measures a team’s efficiency, showing how many points they score per 100 possessions.

Another factor that needs to be talked about is how tough the West was compared to the East.

What if the Suns were in the East? Would they be able to stand out?

This bubble chart with the size of the dot scaled by number of wins shows that the Suns would have been exceptional in the East.

Only one team in the East had a better offensive rating than the Phoenix Suns, the Miami Heat, who had the star trio of LeBron James, Dwyane Wade, and Chris Bosh.

Additionally, it is worth noting that the third-seeded Toronto Raptors finished with the same number of wins as the Suns. Meanwhile, the Atlanta Hawks finished with 38 wins but still made the NBA Playoffs as the 8th seed.

However, the Phoenix Suns did enough to make the playoffs in the West. There were stretches during the season when they were in the playoff picture.

The Suns picked the worst possible time to slump, losing three of their final four games. The losses to the Dallas Mavericks and Memphis Grizzlies proved to cost them. On April 12th, Phoenix fell to the Mavericks 101-98, followed by a 97-91 loss to the Grizzlies just two days later.

Those two teams were competing with the Suns for playoff positioning, and if Phoenix had won both games, we would have seen this Suns team in the 2014 playoffs.
